spacing: base: 4px xs: 4px sm: 8px md: 16px lg: 24px xl: 40px margin-mobile: 20px gutter: 12px --- ## Brand & Style This design system is built for a premium, engineering-focused mobile environment where technical rigor meets impeccable craftsmanship. The brand personality is authoritative yet energetic, balancing the "production-grade" feel of developer tools with the warmth of a high-end consumer product. The aesthetic direction is **Modern-Industrial Minimalism**. It rejects the generic, overly-smooth "AI aesthetic" in favor of high-contrast layouts, precise geometry, and tactical depth. The interface should feel like a physical instrument—solid, responsive, and reliable. To achieve this, we use a sophisticated off-white base to reduce eye strain, punctuated by high-energy orange accents and subtle film grain or noise textures to give digital surfaces a tangible, non-synthetic quality. ## Colors The palette is anchored by a high-contrast relationship between the signature **#FF6200** orange and a refined **#FBF9F9** off-white background. This specific neutral hue removes the clinical harshness of pure white while maintaining maximum legibility. - **Primary:** #FF6200 is used for primary actions and critical status indicators. - **Gradients:** Use the orange-to-yellow gradient sparingly for hero sections, data visualizations, or background ornaments to inject energy. - **Neutrals:** Use a tiered scale of warm grays (e.g., #E8E4E4 for borders, #1A1A1A for primary text) to maintain the "engineered" look without appearing cold. - **Texture:** Apply a 2-3% monochromatic noise overlay on large background surfaces to create a premium, paper-like finish. ## Typography The typography strategy leverages the tension between the literary, authoritative **Newsreader** and the technical, monolinear **Geist**. - **Newsreader** is reserved for headlines, quotes, and editorial moments. It provides a "human" and "premium" contrast to the data-heavy content. - **Geist** handles all functional UI, body text, and labels. Its precise, developer-centric construction reinforces the engineering focus of the application. - Use **tight letter-spacing** on headlines for a compact, editorial look, and **increased tracking** on Geist labels to improve legibility at small sizes. ## Layout & Spacing This design system utilizes a **4px baseline grid** to ensure mathematical precision in all element placements. - **Mobile Layout:** A 4-column fluid grid with 20px outside margins. - **Rhythm:** Use 16px (md) as the default padding for containers and 24px (lg) to separate distinct content blocks. - **Density:** Maintain high information density typical of engineering tools, but use generous vertical whitespace between Newsreader headlines and Geist body text to preserve a "premium" feel. ## Elevation & Depth Depth is achieved through **tonal layering** and **subtle tactile shadows** rather than dramatic blurs. - **Levels:** Surfaces are elevated using 1px borders in #E8E4E4 or by shifting the background to #FFFFFF (Pure White) against the #FBF9F9 base. - **Shadows:** Use extremely low-opacity (2-4%) black shadows with a tight blur radius to make cards feel like they are sitting millimeters off the surface. - **Interaction:** On press, elements should visually "sink" by removing the shadow, reinforcing a mechanical button feel. ## Shapes To balance the technical typography, the shape language uses **full roundedness (Pill-shaped)** for interactive elements. - **Buttons & Chips:** Must use the `ROUND_FULL` (32px+) treatment. This creates a distinct "touchable" contrast against the structured, rectangular layout of data and text. - **Cards:** Use `rounded-lg` (16px) or `rounded-xl` (24px) to contain content, ensuring a softer container for the high-contrast elements inside. - **Inputs:** Maintain the pill-shape for text fields to stay consistent with the primary action buttons. ## Components - **Buttons:** Primary buttons use the #FF6200 background with white text and a full-pill radius. Use a subtle inner-shadow for a "pressed-in" look during active states. - **Chips/Badges:** Use Geist Bold for text within pill-shaped containers. For technical status, use a monochromatic border; for highlights, use the orange-to-yellow gradient. - **Input Fields:** Outlined pill-shaped containers with a 1.5px border. The border shifts to #FF6200 on focus. - **Cards:** Cards should have no visible shadow by default, instead using a thin #E8E4E4 border. Use the noise texture on the card background to differentiate it from the primary app background. - **Navigation:** Use clear, sharp iconography paired with Geist labels. Active states are indicated by the primary #FF6200 orange. - **Data Displays:** Use Geist for all numbers and metrics, ensuring they feel "calculated" and precise.
